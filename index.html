Poshan Tracker

Worked on Poshan Tracker, a mission-critical government platform that supports nutrition governance at national scale. I helped modernize and scale the backend so it could serve millions of beneficiaries with higher reliability and lower operational overhead. The work included shipping high-throughput APIs, moving core workflows toward modular services, and improving database and integration performance for time-sensitive public services. The outcome was a platform that could better support large-scale usage while remaining resilient under real-world demand.

PHPLaravelLaravel LumenFastifyNode.jsAWS DynamoDBAmazon S3RabbitMQ

Impact

  • Own and build the backend APIs powering the Poshan Tracker mobile app, a nationwide MWCD platform serving millions of beneficiaries
  • Led the monolith-to-microservices migration, decomposing the platform into 10+ independently deployable services for faster, safer releases
  • Migrated core data stores to DynamoDB, sustaining millions of records with low-latency reads and writes at scale
  • Architected and shipped the ABDM (Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission) integration module, linking the platform to national digital health infrastructure
  • Restructured high-traffic modules to achieve sub-200ms API response times and ~40% lower server load

How it holds up under load

Scalability

Split critical workflows into independently deployable services so growth in public usage would not force the entire platform to move in lockstep.

Data design

Leveraged DynamoDB for high-throughput operational records and Redis for hot reads so the platform could preserve low latency under heavy traffic.

Reliability

Used asynchronous messaging and container-based deployment to reduce coupling and make failures easier to isolate and recover from.

Operations

Focused on observability and deployment discipline so the platform remained supportable in production with clear service boundaries and healthier release cycles.

How I work

Engineering approach

The principles behind the systems above.

01

Reliability over cleverness

A service that wakes someone at 3 AM isn't finished. I optimize for predictable, observable systems and clear failure modes before I optimize for elegance.

02

Measure, then optimize

I chase real bottlenecks, not guesses. Profiling and metrics come before rewrites — that discipline is how high-traffic modules reached sub-200ms responses and ~40% lower server load.

03

Ship in safe increments

Monolith to 10+ services, data-store migrations, national health-system integrations — decomposed into small, reversible steps so releases stay calm and recoverable.

04

Design for the next engineer

If a system can't be understood and debugged by someone who didn't write it, it isn't production-ready. Clear service boundaries beat clever abstractions.
